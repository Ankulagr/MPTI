Vibration Audit

We extend expertise in conductingMPTI audit at your plant. Based on present schedule and asset health condition, our experts would utilize a ranking process that is objective, quantitative and incorporate ranking criteria for all areas of concern. As business models vary, the criteria and the weighting will be different from plant to plant within a Complex or industry to industry. It becomes important to develop exact weighting & criteria with representation and agreement from all owners of the asset.

In other words you achieve optimal, cost effective plant operation. The objective of providing such approach in your own organization is to increase the reliability and reduce the maintenance cost on rotating equipment. This will guarantee minimizing unscheduled maintenance stoppages by at least 25% in the long run.

We have such expertise for industries like Refinery, Cement, Sugar, Petrochemical, Chemical, Pharmaceutical, Oil & Gas Production Platforms, Rigs and Gas station, Automobile, etc., including CNC & NC machines.

Such Audit carried out abroad in a Petrochemical Complex on a total number of 778 equipments (based on present equipments being monitored) indicated only 621 equipments required Condition Monitoring.

157 equipments did not require regular attention as they were not being used in the present process or used sparingly. There was drastic increase in Non critical equipments and reduction in Critical equipments thereby manpower and time could be better utilized. The Critical equipment got their due attention by the present Rating system. Weekly leveling of equipments was planned and on an average 112 per week were considered (with minimum of 108 and maximum of 118 equipments during each week). This would help the Planning and maintenance departments to allocate officials towards effective use of Proactive maintenance Activity in the Complex.

Chronic / critical equipment got their due attention by such Rating system. Weekly leveling of equipments is planned with minimum and maximum equipments during each week due not differ much. This would help the Planning and maintenance departments to allocate officials towards effective use of Proactive maintenance Activity in the Complex.

It is imperative to re-visit at least once in 6 Months asMPTI Criticality Ranking is dynamic. A spreadsheet or database is required to manage the information. Alternately we guide to develop / procure software and customize to your business model. Such completed ranking helps to prioritize criticality ranking for all types of Condition Monitoring activities, the maintenance strategy and type & periodicity of monitoring to be performed. The overall ranking can be loaded to the CMMS module and be used to help prioritize work orders.

We commenced by setting up ranking criteria for each area, weighting the criteria and reflecting the criteria's relative importance to the organization. The next step was to ensure the criteria are measurable and the whole organization agrees with them. This was followed by a smaller team of knowledgeable personnel ranking each piece of equipment. A spreadsheet or database was used to manage the information. Alternately develop / procure software and customize to your business model.

The completed ranking was used to prioritize the maintenance strategy and type of monitoring is performed. The overall ranking was loaded to the CMMS module and used to help prioritize work orders.

Along with the maintenance officials, we involved the production & Safety officials of each plant and section in order to collect the data required for such audit. The procedure adopted towards Criticality analysis Rating was based on -

  1. Effect on Safety
  2. Effect on Environment
  3. Effect on Production
  4. Effect on Quality
  5. Service Level (effect of Failure of other equipment in the loop)
  6. Operational Period
  7. Elevation (Height from Ground level)
  8. Indication in Control Room
  9. Availability of Stand by equipment
  10. Effect on Production Downtime
  11. Frequency of Failure


Based on the above findings, the equipments were categorized as Most Critical, Critical, and Non Critical. The draft Criticality Rating of each plant was circulated among the owners of the asset in each plant . The Criticality Rating was finalized in discussion with them.
